MINERALS




Why are Minerals Important?

Your body needs minerals to build strong bones and teeth and turn the food you eat into energy. Similar to vitamins, a healthy balanced diet should provide all the minerals your body needs to work properly.


What are Essential Minerals?

Some essential minerals include calcium, iron and potassium. A small amount of these minerals are just as vital to our health as the major minerals, but we don't need large amounts.


Where are minerals found in food?

Minerals are found in foods like cereals, bread, meat, fish, milk, dairy, nuts, fruit (especially dried fruit) and vegetables.


What Happens when you have too many Minerals?

Minerals (especially taken in large doses) can cause side effects such as tooth staining, increased urination, stomach bleeding, uneven heart rate, confusion, and muscle weakness or limp feeling. When taken as directed, multivitamins and minerals are not expected to cause serious side effects.
